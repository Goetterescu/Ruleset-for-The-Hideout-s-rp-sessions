Emis Guideline

The fire department and emergency medical services share the same guideline, they will be adressed as Emis.

    Vehicles of the Emis are only considered as such if they're meeting the following requirements.
    • 1
      Emi vehicles have to be equipped with their specific lighting.
    • 2
      The driver has to be on duty as an EMS-member or fire fighter (use the respectiv command).
    • 3
      The driver or any of their passangers have to be either responding or part of an emergency. Trainings are not an exception, so drivers are not allowed to make use of this section during a simulation or similar.
  • ยง2
    The parking section can be ignored during an emergency.
  • ยง3
    There are four official codes to which any Emi can respond. These have to be forwarded to the chief of the corresponding department before the driver merges into a public road or turns on his lights/horns.
    • Code 0
      Training or other similar activities. No special lights or horns are allowed; No special rights reserved for this code.
    • Code 1
      Calls where one or more people are in the process of dying or close to that. All horns and special lights are allowed. In case of an traffic incident, the driver of the emergency vehicle can not be sued and will not have to pay for any damage.
    • Code 2
      People are in direct danger or critical health. Same rights as above but drivers of Emis can be charged in case of an accident.
    • Code 3
      General calls. No horns, but special lights are allowed. Drivers have to drive cautious and avoid excessiv speeding.
